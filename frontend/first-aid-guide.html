‚ù§Ô∏è CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

  1. Check for responsiveness by tapping and shouting
  2. Call emergency services or ask someone else to call
  3. Position the person on their back on a firm surface
  4. Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest, place the other hand on top
  5. Push hard and fast at least 2 inches deep at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute
  6. Give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions if trained
  7. Continue until help arrives or the person starts breathing normally

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

Only give rescue breaths if you are trained. Hands-only CPR is effective for untrained bystanders.

üîÑ Choking

  1. Determine if the person can speak, cough, or breathe
  2. If they cannot, ask for help and call emergency services
  3. Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist
  4. Make a fist with one hand and place it just above the navel
  5. Grasp your fist with the other hand and give quick, upward thrusts
  6. Repeat until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious
  7. If unconscious, begin CPR and check the mouth for visible objects

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

Do not perform the Heimlich maneuver if the person can still cough, speak, or breathe.

ü©∏ Severe Bleeding

  1. Call emergency services immediately
  2. Have the person lie down and elevate the bleeding area if possible
  3. Wear gloves if available to protect yourself
  4. Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or bandage
  5. If blood soaks through, add another cloth on top without removing the first
  6. Maintain pressure until help arrives
  7. If bleeding is from an arm or leg and uncontrollable, apply pressure to the artery

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

Do not remove deeply embedded objects. Apply pressure around the object instead.

üî• Burns

  1. Remove the person from the source of the burn
  2. Cool the burn with cool (not cold) running water for at least 10 minutes
  3. Remove any jewelry or tight clothing near the burn before swelling occurs
  4. Cover the burn with a clean, dry cloth or sterile bandage
  5. Elevate the burned area above the heart if possible
  6. Give pain relief medication if the person is conscious and able to swallow

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

Never apply ice, butter, or ointments to burns. Do not break blisters that form.

ü¶¥ Fractures

  1. Call emergency services for serious fractures
  2. Stop any bleeding by applying pressure with a clean cloth
  3. Immobilize the injured area using splints or slings if available
  4. Do not attempt to realign the bone or push protruding bones back in
  5. Apply ice wrapped in a cloth to reduce swelling and pain
  6. Monitor for signs of shock and treat accordingly

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

Never move a person with a suspected spinal injury. Wait for professional help.

üòµ Fainting

  1. Loosen tight clothing, especially around the neck
  2. Lay the person down and elevate their legs above heart level
  3. Check breathing and pulse
  4. Ensure fresh air circulation
  5. Splash cool water on the face or gently fan the person
  6. When conscious, give small sips of water
  7. Do not let the person stand up quickly

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

If the person does not regain consciousness within 1-2 minutes, call emergency services.
